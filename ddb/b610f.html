Meanings

mentation

Senses

  • Thought, intellect (Skt. manas; Tib. yid); the mind (Skt. citta; Tib. sems). [Charles Muller; source(s): Nakamura, YBh-Ind, JEBD, Yokoi, Iwanami]
  • Intention, aim, inclination (Skt. āśaya, abhiprāya; Tib. bsam pa). [Charles Muller; source(s): Nakamura]
  • Consideration, mind, thought, idea; the action of the pondering mind. (Tib. nyon yid rnam par shes pa; Pāli manas). In 'eight consciousness' theory (such as in the Yogācāra school), it is a term for the seventh (manas 末那識) consciousness. In this case, the sixth mano consciousness is usually expressed as 識. 〔顯揚論, T 1602.31.480c16-28〕 [Charles Muller]
  • In Sarvāstivādin 說一切有部 theory, the mental faculty (among the six faculties), functioning to cognize. The mind organ—synonymous with 心 and 識. [Charles Muller]
  • As one of the twelve cognitive fields (十二處), the field of mind. [Charles Muller]
  • Deluded thought mistakenly produced in the mind. [Charles Muller]
  • (Skt. kleśa-vijñāna; mati; adhimukti, adhyāśaya, abhiprāya-vaśa, abhiprāyika, abhipreta, abhisaṃdhi, ābhiprāyika, āśā, iccha, icchā, iṣṭa, cetas, chanda, naya, buddhi, buddhin, bhāva, mata, mana-indriya, manasi-kāra, manas-kāra, mano-dhātu, manomaya, mano-ratha, manyanā, mānasa, vyavasāya, saṃcintya, saṃjñā, samādāna, smṛti) [Charles Muller; source(s): Hirakawa]
